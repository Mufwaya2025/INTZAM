2. Definitions

The following terms are used in this policy as defined in the Data Protection Act No. 3 of 2021:

  • Personal Data — any information relating to an identifiable individual, including name, identification number, location data, online identifiers, or factors specific to physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural, or social identity.
  • Sensitive Personal Data — personal data revealing race or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade union membership, genetic data, biometric data, health data, or data concerning a person's sex life or sexual orientation.
  • Processing — any operation performed on personal data, whether or not by automated means, including collection, recording, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion.
  • Data Subject — you, the individual whose personal data is being processed.

3. Personal Data We Collect

3.1 Identity and KYC data

Full name, date of birth, gender, National Registration Card (NRC) number, passport number (for non-Zambian nationals), and a photograph of yourself and your identity document. This data is mandatory for all regulated lending activities under the Financial Intelligence Centre Act No. 46 of 2010 (as amended) and BoZ Anti-Money Laundering Directives.

3.2 Contact data

Phone number, email address, physical residential address, and next-of-kin contact details.

3.3 Employment and financial data

Employer name, job title, payroll number, monthly salary, bank account details (for disbursement and repayment), credit history from credit reference bureaux, existing loan obligations, and other financial information needed to assess creditworthiness.

3.4 Application and transactional data

Loan amount requested, purpose of loan, supporting documents you upload (payslips, employment letters, bank statements), loan repayment history, and records of all communications with IntZam.

3.5 Technical and device data

IP address, browser type, device identifiers, and pages you visit on our website. This data is collected automatically via cookies and similar technologies. See Section 10 for details.

4. How We Collect Your Data

  • Directly from you — when you fill in an enquiry or lead form, apply for a loan, register on the client portal, or contact us by phone, email, or in person.
  • From authorised third parties — credit reference bureaux licensed in Zambia, your employer's payroll department (where applicable), and government registries, where required for identity verification or credit assessment.
  • From the Financial Intelligence Centre (FIC) — where required for AML/CFT compliance.
  • Automatically through digital channels — via cookies and server logs when you browse our website or use the client portal.

6. Sensitive Personal Data

Where the processing of your financial data touches on health information (for example, where you disclose a medical condition as the reason for your loan), we treat this as sensitive personal data. Such data is processed only where strictly necessary for the establishment or exercise of legal claims, or for the performance of obligations under Zambian law. We apply additional safeguards including strict access controls and encryption for all sensitive personal data.

We do not process biometric data for identification purposes at this time. Should this change, we will update this policy and obtain your explicit consent.

7. Sharing Your Personal Data

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your data with:

  • Credit reference bureaux licensed in Zambia — for creditworthiness assessment and credit registry reporting, as required by regulation.
  • Your employer — for payroll deduction arrangements, where you have authorised this in your loan agreement.
  • The Bank of Zambia (BoZ) — for regulatory reporting, supervision, and compliance purposes.
  • The Financial Intelligence Centre (FIC) — for the reporting of Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) and other AML/CFT obligations.
  • The Office of the Data Protection Commissioner (ODPC) — where required by law or in response to a lawful direction.
  • Law enforcement authorities and courts — where required by a valid court order, subpoena, or legal obligation under the Laws of Zambia.
  • Authorised service providers — third parties who provide systems, IT infrastructure, SMS, and other operational services on our behalf, subject to strict data processing agreements and confidentiality obligations.
  • Debt collection agents — in the event of default, where permitted by your loan agreement.

We will not share your personal data with any other third party without your prior written consent, except where required by law.

8. Cross-Border Data Transfers

Zambia's Data Protection Act No. 3 of 2021 requires that personal data be processed and stored on servers or data centres located within Zambia unless specific conditions are met. We are committed to data localisation and use Zambia-based infrastructure for the storage of personal and sensitive personal data wherever practicable.

Sensitive personal data (as defined in Section 2) is never transferred outside Zambia under any circumstances, in compliance with the DPA.

Where any third-party service providers host data outside Zambia, we ensure that such transfers are covered by Commissioner-approved contractual safeguards or other approved mechanisms under Section 72 of the DPA, and that your consent has been obtained where required.

10. Cookies and Website Tracking

Our website uses the following types of cookies:

  • Essential cookies — required for the website to function (e.g., session management, form security). These cannot be disabled.
  • Analytics cookies — used to understand how visitors use our website, in anonymised and aggregated form only. You may decline these via the cookie banner.

We do not use advertising, retargeting, or third-party tracking cookies. You can control cookie preferences through your browser settings. Disabling essential cookies may impair website functionality.

11. Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, loss, alteration, or disclosure. These measures include:

  • Encryption of data in transit (TLS) and at rest.
  • Role-based access controls limiting data access to authorised staff only.
  • Pseudonymisation of data used for analytics and reporting purposes.
  • Regular security assessments and staff training on data protection obligations.
  • Incident response procedures for data breaches.

Data breach notification: In the event of a personal data breach, we will notify the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner within 24 hours of becoming aware of the breach, as required by Section 60 of the DPA. We will notify affected data subjects as soon as practicable where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights and freedoms.

12. Your Rights as a Data Subject

Under Part IX of the Data Protection Act No. 3 of 2021, you have the following rights in relation to your personal data held by IntZam:

  1. Right to be informed — to know that your personal data is being collected, the purpose of collection, the envisaged retention period, and the source of the data.
  2. Right of access — to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you, in an intelligible format, together with information about how it is being processed.
  3. Right to rectification — to request correction of any inaccurate or incomplete personal data we hold about you. We will act on this as soon as practicable.
  4. Right to erasure — to request deletion of your personal data where it is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, where you have withdrawn consent (and no other lawful basis exists), or where processing is unlawful. This right is subject to our legal retention obligations.
  5. Right to object — to object to processing of your personal data, including for direct marketing. We will cease processing upon receiving a valid objection, unless we have compelling legitimate grounds to continue.
  6. Right to restrict processing — to request that we limit processing of your data (e.g., while an accuracy dispute is resolved or a processing objection is pending).
  7. Right to data portability — to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, and to transmit it to another controller, where technically feasible.
  8. Right against automated decision-making — to not be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, that produces legal or similarly significant effects, without human review. You may request that any automated credit decision be reviewed by a qualified member of our team.
  9. Right to withdraw consent — to withdraw consent to processing at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal. Withdrawal of consent for mandatory processing (e.g., KYC) may prevent us from continuing to provide services to you.
  10. Right to lodge a complaint — to lodge a complaint with the Office of the Data Protection Commissioner if you believe your rights under the DPA have been violated.

To exercise any of these rights, submit a written request to hello@intzam.com. We will respond within a reasonable time and in accordance with the DPA. We may ask you to verify your identity before acting on your request.

13. Automated Decision-Making and Credit Scoring

We use automated tools to assist with credit assessment, including evaluating repayment capacity based on your declared income, expenses, and credit bureau data. These tools assist our staff but do not make final lending decisions without human review. You have the right to request human review of any credit decision and to receive an explanation of the principal factors taken into account. Contact us at hello@intzam.com to make such a request.

14. Marketing Communications

We will only send you marketing messages about IntZam's products and services if you have given your prior consent (opt-in). Each marketing message will clearly identify IntZam as the sender and will include a functional opt-out mechanism. You can withdraw marketing consent at any time by emailing hello@intzam.com or by using the unsubscribe link in any marketing email. This is in accordance with the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act No. 4 of 2021.

15. Children's Data

Our services are available only to persons aged 18 years and above. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children under 18. If you believe that a minor's data has been provided to us without appropriate parental or guardian consent, please contact us immediately at hello@intzam.com and we will take steps to delete such data.

16. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, the regulatory environment, or the nature of our services. The most current version will always be available at intzam.com/privacy/ with the effective date shown at the top. Where changes are material, we will notify you by email or via the client portal. Continued use of our services following notification of a material change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
